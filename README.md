## What This Repo Actually Is

**Precinct 92 — Magical Feelings Enforcement** is a code-based creative / satirical project
that imagines a fictional police precinct dedicated to enforcing imaginary statutes about
feelings, vibes, and intangible emotional crimes.

It is **satire**. Think [The Onion](https://www.theonion.com/) applied to the concept of
policing — except as a software repo rather than a news website.

## Physically Verifiable Real-World Analogues

Because this project was called out (correctly) for lacking grounding, here are concrete,
verifiable real-world references it riffs on:

### 1. Real NYC Police Precincts
NYPD actually numbers its precincts. Precinct 90 covers Williamsburg, Brooklyn. Precinct 94
covers Greenpoint. "Precinct 92" does not exist in NYPD's official roster — making it a
deliberate fictional gap.
- **Verify:** [NYPD Precinct Directory](https://www.nyc.gov/site/nypd/bureaus/patrol/precincts-landing.page)

### 2. Satirical Precedent — The Onion
The Onion has published fake law enforcement stories for decades, including fictional
departments and absurdist statutes. Courts have cited The Onion in First Amendment rulings
on satire.
- **Verify:** [The Onion](https://www.theonion.com/)
- **Legal reference:** *Novak v. City of Parma* (6th Cir. 2022) — court used The Onion's
  amicus brief on satirical speech protections.
  [Case summary](https://www.eff.org/cases/novak-v-city-of-parma)

### 3. Real Legal Disclaimer Templates
Parody projects require clear disclaimers under US fair use doctrine (17 U.S.C. § 107).
- **Verify:** [US Copyright Office — Fair Use](https://www.copyright.gov/fair-use/)
- This repo includes [`DISCLAIMER.md`](./DISCLAIMER.md) that follows standard satirical
  disclaimer conventions.
